How to Extend the Life of Your Court Surface

Maintaining a pickleball court is about more than just keeping it looking good. It's about safety, playability, and protecting your significant investment.

Pickleball courts are subjected to extreme weather and intense play. To keep your acrylic surface in top condition, you should follow a regular maintenance schedule. Neglecting small issues can lead to costly repairs down the road.

1. Weekly Cleansing Protocol

Remove leaves, pine needles, and other organic debris. If left on the court, these can cause staining and promote mold growth. Use a soft broom or a leaf blower to keep the surface clear. Weekly pressure washing is also recommended for high-traffic areas.

"A clean court is a fast court. Don't let debris affect your game or your safety. Proactive care is the best way to ensure longevity."

Technical Director, PicklePro

2. Manage Water Drainage

Ensure that water drains off the court properly. Standing water can eventually seep into the sub-base, leading to structural cracks. Check your court after heavy rain to identify any "birdbaths" or low spots that may need professional leveling.

3. Enforce Proper Footwear

Only non-marking tennis or court shoes should be allowed on the surface. Running shoes or cross-trainers often have aggressive tread that can wear down the acrylic texture prematurely, leading to slippery spots.
