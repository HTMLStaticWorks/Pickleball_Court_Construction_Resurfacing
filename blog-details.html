How to Extend the Life of Your Court Surface

Maintaining a pickleball court is about more than just keeping it looking good. It's about safety and playability.

Pickleball courts are subjected to extreme weather and intense play. To keep your acrylic surface in top condition, you should follow a regular maintenance schedule.

1. Weekly Cleaning

Remove leaves, pine needles, and other organic debris. If left on the court, these can cause staining and promote mold growth. Use a soft broom or a leaf blower to keep the surface clear.

"A clean court is a fast court. Don't let debris affect your game or your safety."

2. Manage Water Drainage

Ensure that water drains off the court properly. Standing water can eventually seep into the sub-base, leading to structural cracks. Check your court after heavy rain to identify any "birdbaths" or low spots.

3. Proper Footwear

Only non-marking tennis or court shoes should be allowed on the surface. Running shoes or cross-trainers often have aggressive tread that can wear down the acrylic texture prematurely.
